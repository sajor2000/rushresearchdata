How to Read This Dashboard

  • H-Index — A researcher with h-index h has published at least h papers, each cited at least h times. It captures sustained impact over a career, not just volume.
  • Rush 5yr — Publications from 2021–2026 where at least one author lists a Rush affiliation. This is the best available measure of recent institutional research activity.
  • 2yr Citedness — Average citations per paper published in the last two years. Captures how much attention a researcher’s recent work is attracting from the field.
  • Important: H-indices are not comparable across disciplines. An h-index of 15 in nursing represents a different level of achievement than h = 15 in molecular biology. Any cross-department comparison should account for field-specific citation norms.
  • Data source: Faculty names from the Rush University directory (rushu.rush.edu). Publication metrics from OpenAlex, an open scholarly database. Some faculty may be unmatched or misattributed—see Methodology on the Faculty Directory tab for details.

In Most Departments, a Handful of Faculty Produce the Majority of Research
Select a department. The diagonal line = perfectly equal distribution.
How to read this: The green curve traces cumulative publications from least to most productive faculty. If output were perfectly equal, the curve would follow the diagonal. The more it bows downward, the more concentrated output is in a few people. The Gini coefficient quantifies this: 0 = equal, 1 = one person produces everything. Most academic departments fall between 0.4 and 0.8.
